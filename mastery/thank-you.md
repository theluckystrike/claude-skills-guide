<h1 style="font-size: 2rem; font-weight: 800; margin-bottom: 16px;">You're In.</h1>

<p style="font-size: 1.15rem; color: #555; margin-bottom: 32px;">Your Claude Code Mastery resources are ready. Here's how to get the most out of them.</p>

<hr style="margin: 24px 0;">

<h2>Step 1 — Download Your Resources</h2>

<p>Check your email for the download link. You'll receive a ZIP file with all 16 resources organized into folders:</p>

<pre><code>claude-code-mastery/
├── claude-md-templates/        (8 stack-specific CLAUDE.md files)
├── orchestration-configs/      (3 multi-agent configs with launch scripts)
├── operating-principles/       (NASA Power of 10 for AI development)
└── sprint-templates/           (4 sprint management templates)
</code></pre>

<h2>Step 2 — Start with Your Stack's CLAUDE.md</h2>

<p>Find the template that matches your primary stack. Copy it to your project root as <code>CLAUDE.md</code>. Then:</p>

<ol>
<li>Replace the <code>[PROJECT_NAME]</code> and <code>[URL]</code> placeholders with your project details</li>
<li>Adjust version numbers if your project uses different versions</li>
<li>Read through the "Common Pitfalls" section — these are the issues that waste the most time</li>
<li>Start a new Claude Code session in your project. It will read the CLAUDE.md automatically.</li>
</ol>

<p>You should notice an immediate difference in code quality on your very first session.</p>

<h2>Step 3 — Try an Orchestration Config</h2>

<p>If you have Claude Code Pro or Max, try the <strong>Code Review Fleet</strong> first — it's the fastest to set up:</p>

<ol>
<li>Open a terminal in your project</li>
<li>Open the <code>code-review-fleet.md</code> config</li>
<li>Copy the AGENT-REVIEWER prompt into a Claude Code session</li>
<li>Feed it your latest PR diff</li>
<li>Compare the review quality to what you've been getting manually</li>
</ol>

<h2>Step 4 — Read the Operating Principles</h2>

<p>The NASA Power of 10 guide isn't just theory. The "Enforcement" blocks at the end of each rule are designed to be copied directly into your CLAUDE.md. Pick the rules most relevant to your codebase and add them.</p>
